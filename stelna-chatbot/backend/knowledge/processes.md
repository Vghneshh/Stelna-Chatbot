## 3D Printing

3D Printing is an additive manufacturing process that builds parts layer by layer from a digital CAD model. It is best suited for rapid prototyping and low-volume production under 50 units.

It enables complex internal geometries without tooling. However, mechanical strength may vary depending on print orientation and material selection. Surface finishing may be required for aesthetic or functional needs.

Typical lead time: 2–5 days.


## CNC Machining

CNC Machining is a subtractive manufacturing process that removes material from a solid block using programmed cutting tools.

It supports aluminum, steel, titanium, brass, and engineering plastics. CNC machining provides high precision, tight tolerances, and strong structural parts suitable for functional prototypes and production components.

Limitations include material wastage and difficulty producing complex enclosed internal cavities.

Typical lead time: 7–15 days.


## Injection Molding

Injection Molding is a high-volume plastic manufacturing process where molten plastic is injected into hardened metal molds.

It is ideal for production quantities above 1000 units. It offers excellent surface finish, repeatability, and low per-part cost at scale.

However, tooling cost is high and design modifications after mold creation are expensive.

Typical lead time: 20–35 days.


## Vacuum Casting

Vacuum Casting uses silicone molds derived from master models to produce polyurethane resin replicas.

It is suitable for medium-low volumes between 20 and 100 parts. It provides good surface finish and relatively fast mold production.

Limitations include limited mold life (15–30 parts per mold) and material properties that may differ from injection-molded plastics.

Typical lead time: 15–20 days.


## Vacuum Forming

Vacuum Forming is a thermoforming process where heated plastic sheets are shaped over molds using vacuum pressure.

It is ideal for medium-volume production between 100–5000 units. Common applications include plastic enclosures, automotive panels, trays, and protective covers.

It offers lower tooling cost compared to injection molding but has limited detail resolution and possible thickness variation.

Typical lead time: 10–20 days.


## Sheet Metal Fabrication

Sheet Metal Fabrication involves cutting, bending, forming, and welding metal sheets into structural components.

It supports aluminum, stainless steel, mild steel, copper, and brass. It is ideal for durable enclosures and industrial structural parts.

Limitations include thickness constraints and the need for secondary finishing operations.

Typical lead time: 7–15 days.
